Finance Role Track

Master financial management including chart of accounts, dimensions, journals, and reconciliations. These modules are essential for accountants, controllers, and finance managers working in the wine and spirits industry.

Before You Start: Complete Rounds I-III first to build your foundation in Business Central basics. Finance modules build on core navigation and data entry skills.

Module 1: Configure the Chart of Accounts

Setting Up Your GL Structure

1 hour
Critical for Finance

What you'll learn:

  • Understanding the chart of accounts structure
  • Creating and modifying GL accounts
  • Account categories and subcategories
  • Direct posting vs. totaling accounts
Open Microsoft Learn Module
Wine Industry GL Structure

365WineTrade includes a preconfigured chart of accounts optimized for wine distributors and importers:

  • Inventory accounts: Separate accounts for wine inventory, spirits, and accessories
  • Cost of goods sold: Detailed COGS tracking by product category
  • Excise tax accounts: Federal and state excise tax tracking
  • Landed cost accounts: Freight, duty, and other import costs

Wine Industry Dimensions

Common dimensions used in wine businesses:

  • Wine Region: Track profitability by Bordeaux, Napa, Tuscany, etc.
  • Sales Channel: On-premise, retail, direct-to-consumer, e-commerce
  • Producer/Supplier: Analyze performance by winery or import house
  • Sales Rep: Commission tracking and performance analysis
  • Wine Category: Red, white, sparkling, spirits, accessories

Allocation Use Cases in Wine Business

Common allocation scenarios:

  • Overhead allocation: Distribute warehouse rent across wine categories by case volume
  • Freight allocation: Allocate inbound freight costs to products based on weight or volume
  • Marketing expenses: Split marketing costs across regions or product lines
  • Sales commission: Allocate commission expenses by sales rep and product category

Finance Skills Checklist

After completing this track, you should be able to:

  • Navigate and maintain the chart of accounts
  • Create and configure dimensions for reporting
  • Set up and use journal templates for different transaction types
  • Configure posting groups to automate GL entries
  • Process customer payments and apply them to invoices
  • Process vendor payments and manage payables
  • Import and reconcile bank statements
  • Set up and run allocation processes
  • Understand how transactions flow through the GL
  • Generate financial reports with dimension analysis
