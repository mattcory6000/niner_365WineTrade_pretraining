Wine Warehouse Management Challenges

Wine warehouses have unique requirements:

  • Temperature zones: Different storage requirements for reds, whites, and sparkling wines
  • Vintage tracking: FIFO/FEFO critical for wine rotation and quality management
  • Fragile handling: Glass bottles require careful handling and specialized racking
  • Lot tracking: Each shipment/container typically needs separate lot tracking
  • Mixed cases: Building custom mixed cases requires flexible bin management

365WineTrade's warehouse features are designed to handle these unique wine industry needs.

Wine-Specific Bin Configuration

Best practices for wine warehouse bins:

  • Zone by temperature: Create zones for room temp (reds), cool (whites), and cold (sparkling)
  • Vintage-specific bins: Assign bins by vintage year for easier picking and FIFO management
  • High-value wine storage: Use restricted bins with security requirements for allocated/rare wines
  • Pick faces: Set up primary pick bins for high-velocity wines near packing stations
  • Bulk storage: Configure reserve storage bins for slower-moving vintages and bulk inventory

Tip: Many wine warehouses use bin naming conventions like "COOL-A-01-A" (Zone-Aisle-Bay-Level) for easy navigation.

Wine Inventory Adjustments

Common adjustment scenarios in wine warehousing:

  • Breakage: Recording damaged bottles with reason codes for insurance and loss tracking
  • Sampling: Adjusting inventory for samples sent to customers or used in tastings
  • Evaporation/ullage: Minor losses in barrel programs or long-term storage
  • Cycle counts by vintage: Focus counts on high-value vintages or allocated wines
  • Case to bottle conversions: Adjusting UOM when breaking cases for mixed case building
