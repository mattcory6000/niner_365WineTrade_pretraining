Moving to 365WineTrade

Understanding the differences and transitioning to your live system

What's Different in 365WineTrade

Throughout this QuickStart Guide, you've been learning Business Central using Microsoft's demonstration environment (Cronus). This has given you a solid foundation in navigation, transactions, and core functionality. However, when you log into your organization's live 365WineTrade system, you'll notice important differences designed specifically for the wine and spirits industry.

This page helps you bridge that gap. Understanding what's different - and why - will help you transition confidently from training to production. The good news is that all the core skills you've learned still apply; 365WineTrade enhances Business Central with industry-specific features without changing the fundamental way the system works.

Key Differences: Cronus vs. 365WineTrade

Area Standard BC (Cronus) 365WineTrade
Item Master Generic item fields (description, UOM, category) Wine-specific fields: vintage, varietal, appellation, producer, alcohol content, case configuration, bottle size
Inventory Standard quantity tracking by location Vintage-level tracking, bin location management, allocation pools, aging reports
Sales Orders Basic order entry with pricing Allocation assignment, compliance document generation, vintage substitution suggestions, case/bottle conversion
Pricing Customer price groups and sales prices Vintage-specific pricing, tier pricing by bottle/case/pallet, promotional pricing with date ranges, customer-vintage contracts
Documents Standard invoices and packing slips Compliance-ready documents with TTB requirements, COLAs, vintage details, state-specific formats
Compliance Not applicable Integrated license tracking, state reporting, excise tax calculation, COLA management, shipping compliance validation
Warehouse Basic pick/put-away Wine-optimized workflows: temperature zone management, vintage-specific picking strategies, case break handling
Reports Standard inventory and sales reports Industry-specific: inventory by vintage, sales by varietal/region, depletion reporting, aging analysis, allocation status
Role Centers Generic role centers (sales, purchasing) Wine industry roles: wine trader, compliance manager, warehouse supervisor with relevant KPIs and cues
Important Note

Your organization's 365WineTrade system may also include custom modifications specific to your business processes. Check with your system administrator or Western Computer implementation team to understand your unique configuration.

365WineTrade Feature Previews

Here's what to expect when you encounter these wine-specific features in your production environment:

What it does: Manages limited-availability wines by creating allocation pools that distribute inventory fairly across customers based on purchase history, customer tier, or custom rules.

Where you'll see it:

  • Allocation Pool card - define pools for specific wines and vintages
  • Customer Allocation worksheet - assign quantities to customers
  • Sales Order - allocation assignment and release
  • Allocation Status reports - track remaining pool inventory

Key benefit: Automates the complex process of fairly distributing scarce wines while maintaining customer relationships and maximizing profitability.

What it does: Tracks inventory, pricing, and sales at the vintage level, treating each vintage as a distinct variant with its own lifecycle.

Where you'll see it:

  • Item Card - vintage field and vintage variant management
  • Inventory reports - stock levels by item and vintage
  • Sales documents - vintage selection and automatic substitution suggestions
  • Purchase orders - vintage specification for incoming inventory

Key benefit: Ensures accurate inventory management and prevents vintage mix-ups that could lead to compliance issues or customer dissatisfaction.

What it does: Ensures all sales, shipments, and reporting meet federal and state alcohol beverage regulations.

Where you'll see it:

  • Customer Card - license verification and state restrictions
  • Sales Order - automatic compliance validation (tied house, volume limits)
  • Shipment documents - state-specific format and required information
  • Compliance reporting - excise tax calculations, depletion reports
  • COLA management - track approved labels and formulas

Key benefit: Reduces risk of costly compliance violations and streamlines regulatory reporting across multiple jurisdictions.

What it does: Handles complex case configurations including mixed cases, bottle-to-case conversions, and various pack sizes (6pk, 12pk, etc.).

Where you'll see it:

  • Item Card - case configuration setup and bottle equivalents
  • Sales Order lines - automatic conversion between bottles and cases
  • Pricing - separate price tiers for bottle, case, and pallet quantities
  • Warehouse picks - case break indicators for picker guidance

Key benefit: Eliminates manual conversion errors and supports flexible selling while maintaining accurate inventory counts.

What it does: Provides pre-built reports analyzing your business through the lens of wine industry metrics and dimensions.

Key reports you'll use:

  • Sales by Varietal/Region - understand what's selling by wine type and origin
  • Inventory Aging Analysis - identify slow-moving vintages before they become obsolete
  • Allocation Status - track pool utilization and customer fulfillment
  • Vintage Performance - compare sales across vintages of the same wine
  • Customer Depletion - show what customers are selling through
  • Compliance Dashboard - monitor license status and reporting deadlines

Key benefit: Provides actionable insights specific to wine business decisions without requiring custom report development.

Your Transition Checklist

Use this checklist to guide your first weeks working in the live 365WineTrade system:

Week 1: Orientation
  • Log into your live 365WineTrade environment
  • Explore your role center and note differences from Cronus
  • Review your user permissions with your administrator
  • Locate key pages you'll use daily (Tell Me search)
  • Review your organization's naming conventions for items
  • Understand your workflow for common tasks
Week 2: Data Familiarization
  • Review customer records relevant to your role
  • Explore item master data and wine-specific fields
  • Understand current inventory levels and locations
  • Review pricing structures and customer tiers
  • Examine historical sales orders or documents
  • Identify any data issues to discuss with your team
Week 3: Transactions
  • Process your first transactions with supervision
  • Practice using wine-specific features (vintage, allocations)
  • Generate and review reports relevant to your role
  • Test document printing/emailing (invoices, etc.)
  • Ask questions about any confusing processes
  • Document your personal notes and shortcuts
Week 4: Independence
  • Handle transactions independently with confidence
  • Customize your workspace (personalization)
  • Set up frequently-used filters and views
  • Identify opportunities for process improvement
  • Help onboard other new users if applicable
  • Schedule follow-up training on advanced topics
Ongoing Best Practices
  • Regularly review Microsoft Learn modules to deepen your knowledge
  • Attend monthly user group meetings or training sessions
  • Stay current with 365WineTrade release notes and new features
  • Share tips and shortcuts with colleagues
  • Provide feedback to administrators about system improvements
  • Maintain documentation of your unique processes and workflows
